Projects Made

Physical Inactivity

Participating in the Physical Inactivity Project at Rajarata University was a valuable turning point in my academic journey. Working with university peers to promote active lifestyles allowed me to understand the root causes of physical inactivity. I learned that promoting physical activity is not just about encouraging exercise but also it’s about addressing sedentary behaviour. Through this project I improved my skills in conducting discussion, engaging participants, data collection and evaluating changes. Seeing students gradually adopt active daily routines was a meaningful success.

Improving overall wellbeing of the community in Bogahayaya

One of the most impactful experiences during my BSc in Health Promotion was the community based project in Bogahayaya where we worked with 171 families to address five major health issues. This project challenged me to apply theoretical knowledge in a real life setting. At first I struggled with building relationship in an unfamiliar community but over time I developed stronger communication and facilitation skills. Engaging directly with communities helped me realize the value of empathy, cultural sensitivity, and active listening. Through this project I improved my ability to communicate effectively with others, solve problems creatively and provide leadership appropriately.
